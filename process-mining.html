Why Not Use a Single AI Model?

The discovered process contains numerous alternative transitions, rework loops, and exceptional paths. This complexity makes it difficult to model the entire admission workflow using a single end-to-end AI model while maintaining transparency and auditability.

Instead of treating admissions as one global prediction problem, the proposed digital twin decomposes the workflow into discrete states connected by decision junctions. Each junction is represented by a small interpretable decision tree that determines the next process transition using administrative variables and AI-generated evidence.

This state-centric approach transforms a highly complex workflow into a collection of local decision problems. As a result, individual transition rules remain understandable, auditable, and easier to align with institutional policies.
