Video branch

From mock interview capture to multimodal interview evidence.

The video branch starts with a user-friendly Colab notebook that records a mock interview or redirects to mockup data. From there, FAIR-VID splits interview video into audio, transcripts, transcript grades, frame-level evidence, and non-verbal indicators before later decision models consume the outputs.

Entry notebook

The notebook itself explains the branch split

RecordVideo.ipynb is the demo entry point for the paper and links to the next video-processing notebook plus the parallel document branch. This exposes the FAIR-VID branching logic from the start.

  • Mock interview recording or alternative mockup data.
  • Audio extraction and speech transcription as explicit artifacts.
  • Transcript grading for relevance, clarity, authenticity, and AI-script markers.
  • Visual and behavioral analysis as a separate stage rather than a merged black box.
Branch scope

Why the video pipeline is decomposed into small agents

Admissions interviews are asynchronous, multilingual, and culturally diverse. The paper and notebook chain treat the interview as a sequence of evidence transformations so that transcription quality, behavioral indicators, and authenticity concerns can be inspected separately instead of being merged into one opaque score.

Language

Speech first

Whisper-based transcription is central because understanding what the applicant says is the core admissions task. The notebooks are designed for non-native English accents typical in international admissions.

Behavior

Visual evidence remains separate

Frame extraction, face and motion analysis, and forensic cues are handled as separate components so framework users and paper readers can distinguish transcript-driven conclusions from visual cues.

Transparency

Promptable interview grading

The transcript-grading script fetches its evaluator prompt from the FAIR-VID prompts repository and stores per-transcript outputs rather than only final scores.

Pipeline

Visible stages in the current notebook and Python chain

1

Mock interview capture

The paper demo begins with RecordVideo.ipynb. Framework users and paper readers can either record a real response or switch to the linked notebook that downloads pre-recorded mock data.

2

Audio extraction and transcription

The next notebooks and scripts convert video to audio, then audio to transcripts. FAIR-VID exposes these artifacts because later grading depends on the transcript text rather than a hidden latent representation.

extract_audio_linux.py transcribe_audio_linux.py
3

Transcript evaluation

The transcript grading notebook and Python module evaluate relevance, clarity, naturalness, and markers of AI-generated or scripted delivery using prompt-driven JSON outputs.

video_interview_transcript_grade_ollama.py JSON outputs
4

Visual behavior and frame evidence

The notebook chain then moves to representative frames, visual descriptions, and MediaPipe-oriented face or motion analysis. This is where non-verbal evidence becomes explicit rather than implied.

Paper context

The video branch is also a student-facing transparency artifact

  • The larger draft frames mock interviews as a way for students to inspect AI feedback over repeated attempts.
  • This turns the digital twin into both an evaluation system and a learning environment.
  • Outputs such as transcripts, text evaluations, and frame-level evidence are available for inspection and review.
Operational note

This branch is not only about scoring fluency

FAIR-VID treats interviews as a source of verbal and non-verbal evidence. The objective is not a single personality score; it is an inspectable collection of signals that later decision rules can use together with documents and structured application data.
